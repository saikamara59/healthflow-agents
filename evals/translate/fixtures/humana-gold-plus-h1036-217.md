## Monthly Premium, Deductible and Limits
Monthly plan premium: $0. You must continue to pay your Medicare Part B premium.
Medical deductible: this plan does not have a medical deductible.
Pharmacy (Part D) deductible: this plan has a $0 deductible.
Maximum out-of-pocket: $4,150 in-network. This is the most you will pay in copays
and coinsurance for covered Part A and Part B services in a calendar year.

## Doctor Visits
Primary Care Provider (PCP): $0 copay at the PCP's office. Telehealth: $0 copay.
Specialist: $10 copay at the specialist's office. Telehealth: $10 copay.
Your PCP coordinates your care with specialists through a referral. Certain
services may require preauthorization.

## Emergency and Urgent Care
Emergency services at an emergency room: $140 copay. If you are admitted to the
same hospital within 24 hours for the same condition, you do not have to pay your
share of the cost for the emergency care. When placed in observation, the member
pays the observation cost-share instead of the emergency room cost-share.
Physician and professional services at the emergency room: $0 copay.
Urgently needed services: $5 copay at an urgent care center; $5 copay via telehealth.

## Inpatient Hospital Coverage
This plan covers an unlimited number of days for an inpatient stay.
You pay $175 copay per day for days 1 through 5, and $0 copay per day for days 6
through 90.

## Ambulance
Ground ambulance: $245 copay per date of service.
Air ambulance: 20% of the cost.

## Outpatient Prescription Drugs
Costs shown are for in-network retail pharmacies, 30-day supply, before you reach
the $2,000 annual out-of-pocket limit.
Tier 1 (Preferred Generic): $0 copay.
Tier 2 (Generic): $0 copay.
Tier 3 (Preferred Brand): $35 copay.
Tier 4 (Non-Preferred Drug): 48% of the cost.
Tier 5 (Specialty Tier): 33% of the cost.
You won't pay more than $35 for a one-month (up to 30-day) supply of each
plan-covered insulin product, regardless of cost-sharing tier.
After your total out-of-pocket costs reach $2,000, you pay $0 for plan-covered
Part D drugs for the rest of the calendar year.

## Dental Services
Medicare-covered dental: $10 copay.
Mandatory supplemental dental benefit (in-network): $0 copay for scaling and root
planing (deep cleaning) up to 1 per quadrant every 3 years; $0 copay for a
comprehensive oral evaluation up to 1 every 3 years; $0 copay for bitewing and
intraoral x-rays. Benefits are offered on a calendar-year basis and any unused
amount expires at the end of the year.

## Hearing Services
Medicare-covered hearing exam: $10 copay.
Mandatory supplemental hearing benefit (in-network): $0 copay for a fitting,
evaluation, or routine hearing exam up to 1 per year. Hearing aids: $199 copay for
each Value Technology hearing aid, $699 copay for each Advanced Technology hearing
aid, and $1,299 copay for each Premium Technology hearing aid, up to 1 per ear per
year. Includes a 1-year warranty and a 1-month battery supply.

## Vision Services
Eyewear after cataract surgery: $0 copay.
Medicare-covered diabetic eye exam: $0 copay.
Medicare-covered vision services: $10 copay.
